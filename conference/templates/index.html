REGISTER

The Women in Computing Conference is organized by volunteers who are a combination of students, lecturers and women from the industry.

The conference is co-organized with Women Tech Makers, Google, University of Namibia and the Namibia University of Science and Technology. The conference is also partially sponsored by Google and forms part of the global Anita Borg Birthday Celebrations that are taking place around the world. Anita Borg was a technologist who relentlessly fought to ensure that technology has a positive impact on people’s lives and founded the Anita Borg Institute. You can read more about her at http://anitaborg.org/about-us/about-anita-borg/.

Each year we attract ~200 women interested or in computing and related fields. This includes, high school students, industry women, university students and staff. Topics presented focus mainly on retention, attracting and introducing women and girls to the computing field and providing role models and mentors. This year’s focus is on entrepreneurial initiatives and innovations done by women and girls across the country.
